Motivation

This piece of writing is the fruit of my experience before comming to Brazil and during making my master's studies and working in the BIPC project. Both parts of my life have contributed to this document. I think I have a lot to write on this topic because I ask myself daily whether I can do things better.

If your work has to do with a computer, chances are that you can improve your performance and make your life easier by rearranging your workflow and using the right tools.

I believe that everybody can create their own productivity method. For that, you have you learn from your own experience and write.

Introduction

Your physical order influences your digital order. Most of the ideas of physical order apply to digital order. Order is a key factor for productivity. Avoid having pending activities. Deliver everything on time.

Physical productivity

Your productivity starts even since you wake up.

At work

  • Start by cleaning your desk and if possible, your near workspace.

    • Have the least number of things on it. Keep a clean and tidy space before starting working on the computer. During your work, store the things you are no longer using.

  • Store all unnecessary things.
  • Clean the desk.

    • Digital productivity

    Working on the computer is easier if you have a script of what you will do. Write down what you want to do.

    The steps Starting work, During work, and After work are parts of a loop.

    Starting work

    Start your desktop clean.

    Every time you start working on a new or old project, guarantee the following:

  • Quick and easy access to the project's folder and its parts
  • Clean up the project's folder: Delete the files you needed only once and archive the files you may reuse in the future.
  • Give goog names to your files so that they can be easily found.

    • During work

  • Focus on one thing at a time.
  • Use minimalist apps.
  • Use the minimum number of apps possible.
  • Have the minimum number of apps and tabs open: Close immediately the ones you are not using.
  • Make pauses during work (the 30 min rule, method invented by me).
  • Store things on your desktop you are not using in your pauses (15 min).

    • After work

  • Learn from your own experience.
  • Optimize your access to current or new project's folder you will work later: Minimum number of clicks or time to access.

    • The 30 minute rule

    It's a sequence of 30 min studying and doing a 15-min interval. Every sequence of work lasts 45 min. The 15 min can be used for organizing your desk. Don't use it for checking the phone.
